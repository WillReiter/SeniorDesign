Motion Prediction using Artificial Intelligence

About the project

Abstract

Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning have allowed for a new generation of problems to be solved. Prediction and representation of motion is a crucial application that A.I. We have chosen to focus on near-future weather prediction (nowcasting) which views the task in a sequential context as opposed to temporal context. This differentiation is critical to our approach to this project. We have studied, trained, and tested our TrajectoryGRU deep learning algorithm and explored the results in three different domains in the United States.

Project Background

Prior papers have been written on the topic and propose the use of a convolutional long short-term memory (ConvLSTM) model and deep convolutional neural network (CNN). These models fall short due to the large amounts or radar data required for proper function. Dr. Haonan Chen, our supervising faculty member, has done extensive research into the topic of nowcasting precipitation and has proven that it is possible to use one location model for another location with a limited amount of frames from the new location to be predicted.

Constraints

In the world of Machine Learning, data is your biggest asset. Most people believe the quantity to be the limiting factor, but our approach to weather prediction attempts to disprove that. In our case, hardware and time of simulation will be the main constraints of this project. Intense simulations of complex data require certain hardware to complete in a timely manner. To counteract this, we have chosen to use Google Colab to execute our TrajGRU model. Google Colab is a cloud service that allows users to take advantage of Google hardware remotely.

Goals

Improve upon the commonly used based convolution neural-network (CNN) model, by moving to a CNN-MMD or CNN-FT to provide a flexible and adaptable nowcasting model. Almost all deep-learning based models rely on large sets of localized data to provide accurate predictions of precipitation. Our goal is to remove that dependency and use a transfer learning model to provide real time predictions.
